How Arch works
Native
1.1 The foundation

It never stops being Bitcoin.

Your Bitcoin is a real coin in Bitcoin's own ledger — a UTXO, not an IOU on someone else's. When it moves, the result lands in a Bitcoin block, like any other payment.

FastProgrammable
1.2 The execution layer

A chain that speaks Bitcoin natively.

Apps fire transactions; Arch sequences them into blocks every 180 ms and executes them against real Bitcoin UTXOs. It even has machinery for Bitcoin reorgs — so its record stays consistent with Bitcoin itself.

DecentralizedNative
1.3 The cryptography

There's no single key to hold.

If Arch can move your Bitcoin, who holds the key? Nobody. The authority to move a coin lives only as shares split across Arch's validators. No single party, Arch included, can move anything alone. On-chain it looks like an ordinary Bitcoin spend, because that's what it is.

FastNative
1.4 Settlement

Fast where it needs to be fast. Final where it needs to be final.

Developers can choose when to stay fast on Arch or make things final on Bitcoin. Native Bitcoin, moving faster than Bitcoin alone ever could, then settling with Bitcoin's own finality.

Financial primitives Arch unlocks
Programmable
2.1 The missing primitive

Bitcoin could always send value. It just couldn't pool it.

A pool is shared capital many people can use at once — most markets are built on them. Bitcoin can't support them on its own; Arch can, while your Bitcoin stays native. Pooling real UTXO states unlocks AMMs and more efficient markets — the line between "two people can trade" and "a market anyone can trade against."

ProgrammableDecentralizedNative
2.2 Enforceable collateral

Collateral you can actually enforce — on Bitcoin.

A loan market only works if the lender can seize the collateral without the borrower's consent. Bitcoin can't do it alone, and wrapping it adds another layer of uncertainty. Arch enforces mechanically and settles to Bitcoin, so the borrower can't yank it. Moving that coin takes the validators' signature, not theirs.

FastNative
2.3 Real liquidations

A liquidation that's both on time and final.

A late liquidation isn't slow — it's a loss. On a ten-minute chain you can't promise you'll be on time. Arch detects a breach, locks the price at signing, and broadcasts the closeout in under three seconds — then settles it on Bitcoin.

2.4 The unlock

Lending on Bitcoin — for the first time, natively.

Native settlement made the collateral enforceable. Speed made the liquidations real. Together they make pooled lending possible on Bitcoin itself — no wrapping, no bridge, no custodian. That wasn't possible before. It is now.
