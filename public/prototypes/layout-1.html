          Financial primitives Arch unlocks
          2.1 The missing primitive

          Bitcoin could always send value. It just couldn't pool it.

          A pool is shared capital many people can use at once — most markets are built on them.

            Only on Arch

            Bitcoin can't support them on its own; Arch can, while your Bitcoin stays native.

            What it unlocks

            Pooling real UTXO states unlocks AMMs and more efficient markets — the line between “two people can trade” and “a market anyone can trade against.”

            2.2 Enforceable collateral

            Collateral you can actually enforce — on Bitcoin.

            A loan market only works if the lender can seize the collateral without the borrower's consent.

              The old way

              Bitcoin can't do it alone, and wrapping it adds another layer of uncertainty.

              The Arch way

              Arch enforces mechanically and settles to Bitcoin, so the borrower can't yank it. Moving that coin takes the validators' signature, not theirs.

              2.3 Real liquidations

              A liquidation that's both on time and final.

              A late liquidation isn't slow — it's a loss.

                The problem

                On a ten-minute chain you can't promise you'll be on time.

                On Arch

                Arch detects a breach, locks the price at signing, and broadcasts the closeout in under three seconds — then settles it on Bitcoin.

                2.4 The unlock

                Lending on Bitcoin — for the first time, natively.

                Native settlement made the collateral enforceable. Speed made the liquidations real. Together they make pooled lending possible on Bitcoin itself — no wrapping, no bridge, no custodian. That wasn't possible before. It is now.
