1.2 The execution layer

A chain that speaks Bitcoin natively.

Apps fire transactions; Arch sequences them into blocks every 180 ms and executes them against real Bitcoin UTXOs. It even has machinery for Bitcoin reorgs — so its record stays consistent with Bitcoin itself.

FastProgrammable
1.3 The cryptography

There's no single key to hold.

If Arch can move your Bitcoin, who holds the key? Nobody. The authority to move a coin lives only as shares split across Arch's validators. No single party, Arch included, can move anything alone.

Decentralized
A movement happens only when enough validators sign together — never a single signer, never Arch alone. Decentralized
1.4 Settlement

Fast where it needs to be fast. Final where it needs to be final.

Developers can choose when to stay fast on Arch or make things final on Bitcoin. Native Bitcoin, moving faster than Bitcoin alone ever could, then settling with Bitcoin's own finality.

FastNative
Many fast Arch blocks above; one slow Bitcoin block below — settle whenever you need finality. FastNative
02 Financial primitives Arch unlocks
2.1 The missing primitive

Bitcoin could always send value. It just couldn't pool it.

A pool is shared capital many people can use at once — most markets are built on them. Bitcoin can't support them alone; Arch can, while your Bitcoin stays native. The line between "two people can trade" and "a market anyone can trade against."

ProgrammableFast
Pooling real UTXO states unlocks AMMs and more efficient markets — natively. ProgrammableFast
Arch enforces mechanically and settles to Bitcoin — moving that coin takes the validators' signature, not the borrower's. DecentralizedProgrammable
2.2 Enforceable collateral

Collateral you can actually enforce — on Bitcoin.

A loan market only works if the lender can seize the collateral without the borrower's consent. Bitcoin can't do it alone, and wrapping adds another layer of uncertainty. Arch enforces mechanically and settles to Bitcoin, so the borrower can't yank it.

DecentralizedProgrammable
2.3 Real liquidations

A liquidation that's both on time and final.

A late liquidation isn't slow — it's a loss. On a ten-minute chain you can't promise you'll be on time. Arch detects a breach, locks the price at signing, and broadcasts the closeout in under three seconds — then settles it on Bitcoin.

FastProgrammable
Breach to broadcast in under three seconds, then settled with Bitcoin finality. FastProgrammable
2.4 The unlock

Lending on Bitcoin — for the first time, natively.

Native settlement made the collateral enforceable. Speed made the liquidations real. Together they make pooled lending possible on Bitcoin itself — no wrapping, no bridge, no custodian. That wasn't possible before. It is now.
